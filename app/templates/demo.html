How It Works

A walkthrough of the AI-powered guidance system — from symptom description to resolution playbook.

How the AI Generates Guidance

Every time a specialist searches, the system pulls all resolved tickets from the database and hands them to Claude as context. Claude reads your team's real history and reasons over it to produce guidance — so as more incidents are resolved and documented, the quality of future guidance improves automatically.

1

Specialist describes the symptoms

Plain language — no form fields. Include the application name, exact error message, and who is affected. The more specific, the more targeted the guidance.

2

All resolved tickets are retrieved from the database

Every ticket that was previously resolved — with its title, category, resolution, and resolution notes — is pulled from the knowledge base and included in the prompt sent to Claude.

3

Claude reads the past cases and identifies what matches

Claude reads the symptom description alongside all the past resolved tickets. It identifies which past cases are similar and uses those resolutions to generate one or more actionable solutions — not a generic answer, but guidance grounded in what actually worked before.

4

Specialist sees a step-by-step playbook + the source tickets

The results page shows a prioritized action plan alongside the specific past tickets Claude referenced — so the specialist can verify the reasoning, drill into the original work notes, and judge whether the guidance applies.

5

The system improves as more tickets are resolved

Every ticket resolved with detailed resolution notes is added to the pool. The next time a similar issue comes in, Claude has that case to draw from — combining patterns across multiple past incidents to suggest the most likely solution. Better documentation = better future guidance.

Incident Lifecycle

Open

Ticket is created and assigned to a team. SLA timer starts. Specialist reviews and begins investigation.

In Progress

Specialist is actively working the issue. Work notes document every investigation step — what was checked, what was ruled out, what was escalated and why.

Pending (Vendor / Approval / Another Team)

Ticket is waiting on an external dependency. A sub-ticket may be opened for another team (e.g. Network, Fund Services, API Platform). SLA clock continues.

Resolved

Issue is fixed and confirmed by the user. Resolution notes document exactly what was done — this is what future AI guidance is built from. Quality notes = better future guidance.

Closed

User confirmed resolution and ticket is closed. Historical record is preserved in the knowledge base.

Key Business Rules the AI Enforces

  • •Manager approval required before granting any permissions — the AI will always surface this requirement when access or entitlement changes are involved.
  • •Sub-tickets for third-party systems — when the resolution requires action from another team (Fund Services, Network, Vendor), the AI specifies exactly what system to use and what information to include.
  • •Never disable SSL verification — the AI explicitly warns against this as a security risk even when pressed for a quick workaround.
  • •Secure credential delivery only — API keys and credentials are always delivered via secure channels, never plain email or chat.
  • •Escalate P1 immediately — multiple teams blocked = P1. The AI will set priority and flag escalation path.
